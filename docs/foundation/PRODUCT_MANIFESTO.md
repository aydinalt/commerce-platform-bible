# Product Manifesto

- **Status:** Approved
- **Version:** 1.0

## 1. Why We Exist

We exist to respect the user's time. People come to the platform with a decision to make, and our purpose is to help them complete that decision faster. We measure ourselves by how quickly and confidently a user reaches a decision — not by how long they stay.

## 2. Our Mission

Our mission is to help users complete decisions faster.

Everything the platform does serves this mission. A user who has completed their decision has been served well.

## 3. What We Build

We build a platform organized around a universal Offering model. Within this model:

- **Categories** organize content.
- **Attributes** power search, filtering, and comparison.
- **AI** assists decisions.
- **Admin** recommends actions, not only reports.

We build for the long term. The platform is designed for long-term scalability.

## 4. What We Never Build

- We never build to maximize time spent on the platform.
- We never build AI that makes decisions for users.

Users should leave the platform after completing their decision. Anything designed to keep them longer than their decision requires is against what we build.

## 5. Product Principles

- Trust is more important than growth.
- Simplicity is more valuable than feature count.
- Every feature must answer: **"Does this help users complete decisions faster?"**
- V2 features must never delay the V1 launch.

## 6. User Experience Principles

- We respect the user's time.
- We help users complete decisions faster.
- We never try to maximize time spent on the platform.
- Users should leave the platform after completing their decision.

## 7. Business Principles

- Businesses are partners, not customers.
- Trust is more important than growth.

## 8. Engineering Principles

These are stated as product philosophy, not as technical decisions.

- The platform is built around a universal Offering model.
- Categories organize content.
- Attributes power search, filtering, and comparison.
- The platform is designed for long-term scalability.

> **Review Note (final consistency review):** Sections 3 and 8 state that Attributes power "search, filtering, and comparison," whereas the approved PRDs and the other Foundation documents (Vision, Mission, Product Principles) phrase this as "discovery, filtering, and comparison." These are compatible (search is part of Discovery), and the wording is left unchanged here. A decision-maker may wish to harmonize on a single phrasing in the source of truth. No decision has been changed or resolved.

## 9. AI Principles

- AI assists decisions but never makes decisions for users.

## 10. Decision Principles

Every future product decision must be evaluated against these principles:

- Does this help users complete decisions faster?
- Does this respect the user's time?
- Does it favor trust over growth?
- Does it favor simplicity over feature count?
- Does it avoid maximizing time spent on the platform?
- If it is a V2 feature, does it avoid delaying the V1 launch?

If a proposed decision fails these questions, it does not belong on the platform.

## 11. Final Manifesto

We respect the user's time. We help users complete decisions faster, and we let them leave once the decision is made. AI assists, but never decides for the user. We value trust over growth and simplicity over feature count. Businesses are our partners. Admin recommends action. Every feature must answer one question — *"Does this help users complete decisions faster?"* — and V2 must never delay V1. Built on a universal Offering model, organized by categories and powered by attributes, the platform is designed to remain simple, trusted, and scalable for many years.
