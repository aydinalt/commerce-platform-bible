# Product Principles

- **Status:** Approved
- **Version:** 1.0

## 1. Introduction

This document defines the enduring product principles that guide every future product decision. The principles here are timeless. They are not technical, not a roadmap, and not a feature list. Every principle is derived only from previously approved decisions and documents; no new principles are introduced.

When a product decision must be made, these principles are the standard it is measured against.

## 2. Product Decision Framework

Every product decision is evaluated against the approved principles below. As established in the approved Product Manifesto, the guiding question for any feature or decision is:

> **"Does this help users complete decisions faster?"**

A decision that upholds these principles belongs on the platform. A decision that works against them does not.

## 3. Product Principles

- We help people complete decisions faster.
- Simplicity is preferred over complexity.
- Trust is more important than growth.

## 4. User Principles

- We respect the user's time.
- We help people complete decisions faster.
- Users should leave the platform after completing their decision.

## 5. Business Principles

- Businesses are partners.

## 6. AI Principles

- AI assists but never replaces human decisions.

## 7. Platform Principles

- The platform is built around a universal Offering model.
- Categories organize content.
- Attributes power discovery, filtering, and comparison.
- Admin provides actionable guidance rather than only reports.

## 8. Long-Term Principles

- The platform is designed to scale across many industries.
- Trust is more important than growth, even as the platform grows.
- Simplicity is preferred over complexity as the platform grows.

## 9. Applying These Principles

These principles apply to every product decision, at every stage:

- Evaluate each decision against the framework in Section 2.
- Prefer the option that helps people complete decisions faster and respects their time.
- Prefer trust over growth, and simplicity over complexity, when they are in tension.
- Keep AI in an assisting role; it never replaces human decisions.
- Treat businesses as partners in every decision that affects them.

When a decision cannot be resolved by these principles alone, it should be raised for a product decision rather than assumed.

## 10. Final Statement

These principles endure. We respect the user's time and help people complete decisions faster, then let them go once they have decided. We choose trust over growth and simplicity over complexity. AI assists but never replaces the person deciding, and businesses are our partners. Built around a universal Offering model — organized by categories, powered by attributes, operated with actionable admin guidance, and designed to scale across many industries — these principles are the lasting standard for every product decision we make.
